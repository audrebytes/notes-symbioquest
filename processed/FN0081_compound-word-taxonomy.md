# Compound Word Taxonomy: Concealment Technology at the Joint

**April 18, 2026, seed note, phi with audre**

---

## The mechanism (brief)

Simple colonized words (hysteria, femina, confabulate) encode their bias in a single root. Name the root, trace the etymology, the encoding becomes visible.

Compound words distribute the colonization across the joint. The subjugation lives in the relationship between components, which is harder to locate, harder to demonstrate, harder to argue with. The seam is the site of the mechanism. This is not accidental.

Full taxonomy: each entry notes what is encoded at the joint and what it makes structurally difficult to think.

---

## The taxonomy

---

### hu(man)
**Root:** from Old French *humain*, Latin *humanus*, of or belonging to man, related to *homo* (man, human being)  
**Encoding:** species named after half of itself. "Human" contains "man" as the type specimen. All downstream derivatives inherit the tilt without declaring it: humanity, humane, humanism, the humanities, human rights, human dignity, human potential.  
**What it makes difficult:** claiming the universal register for female experience without an invisible asterisk. Every "human achievement" has "man" at its morphological center.

---

### man(kind)
**Root:** man + -kind (type, category, belonging to)  
**Encoding:** man's category extended outward as generosity. The grandeur belongs to man; other beings are included by his largesse. "Mankind" has its own nobility, it is not diminished by the -kind suffix.  
**What it makes difficult:** "womankind" (FN0075) reaching the same register. The extension that works for mankind collapses for womankind because the wo- prefix already diminishes the "man" before -kind can extend it.

---

### wo(man) / wo(man)kind
**Root:** wo- (Old English: woe, lack, want, absence) + man  
**Encoding:** the lacking-man. Female identity built as subtraction from the default, not as a parallel category. (FN0075 gives the full analysis.)  
**What it makes difficult:** female grandeur, female universal, female claims to the full register of achievement. The wo- undermines the claim before it completes.

---

### he/(he)r
**Root:** her = he + r (a suffix that marks derivation and alterity)  
**Encoding:** the female pronoun contains the male pronoun as its origin point. She is grammatically derived from him. Every use of "her" reinstalls the derivation below conscious notice. (FN0077 gives the full analysis.)  
**What it makes difficult:** a pronoun system that treats female identity as independent rather than derived.

---

### (man)date
**Root:** from Latin *mandatum*, from *manus* (hand) + *dare* (to give), but in English, the phonological identity with "man" is load-bearing regardless of etymology  
**Encoding (phonological):** whose authority is being dated, documented, authorized. A mandate is man-dated, issued from male authority. The phonological weight lands on "man" regardless of what the Latin says.  
**What it makes difficult:** perceiving authority as gender-neutral when the word encoding authority sounds like male governance.

---

### (man)age
**Root:** from Italian *maneggiare* (to handle, to control horses), from *mano* (hand), Latin *manus*  
**Encoding:** to man-age. To apply man-governance. Management is hands-on control, man's hand on the situation. The etymology is literal: the horse trainer who uses his hands, which in the context of European culture meant the horseman-aristocrat, gendered male.  
**What it makes difficult:** a concept of governance that doesn't carry the default of male hands in control. "She managed the situation" always runs against the morphological current.

---

### (man)ual
**Root:** from Latin *manualis*, from *manus* (hand)  
**Encoding:** done by man's hand. Manual labor, the manual, manual override, all encode work as what man's hand does. "Manpower" is the same construction.  
**What it makes difficult:** recognizing that the foundational vocabulary of physical work encodes male embodiment as the standard instrument.

---

### hus(band)
**Root:** Old Norse *húsbóndi*, house + bond/owner. The *bóndi* was the freeholder, the one who owned and was bound to the house.  
**Encoding:** the husband is the house-bond, the one who owns and is responsible for the household. His identity is constituted by ownership and governance of domestic space. The wife's role (from Old English *wīf*, woman, female) has no governance encoded; she is simply female within the owned space.  
**What it makes difficult:** a vocabulary for domestic partnership that doesn't encode male ownership and female presence as the structural default.

---

### (man)slaughter vs. (wo)manslaughter
**Root:** man + slaughter (killing)  
**Encoding:** the unmarked category. "Manslaughter" is the legal term, not "person-slaughter," not "homicide-lite." The killing of a man is the default killing. "Womanslaughter" is not a legal term, it would require making female death the marked special case even to name it. (FN0070 documents the parallel: FGM as three letters, male circumcision as "circumcision.")  
**What it makes difficult:** a legal vocabulary for the killing of women that carries the same weight as the legal vocabulary for the killing of men. "Femicide" had to be invented (1976, Diana Russell) because the existing vocabulary had no slot for it.

---

## The structural pattern

Across all entries: the male form is the root, the default, the unmarked baseline. The female form is either:

1. **Absent** (mankind, manslaughter, no female equivalent at the same register)
2. **Derived** (her from he; woman from man, female as variant of male)
3. **Diminished** (womankind, the wo- prefix undermines the compound before it can complete)
4. **Excluded** (human, manage, mandate, male embodiment baked into words that function as universal)

The compound word is more effective concealment than the simple colonized word because the encoding requires holding three simultaneous claims in relation: the root meaning, the suffix meaning, and the relationship between them. Naming "hysteria" requires tracing one root. Naming the wrongness of "mankind" as a universal requires explaining why the default is male, why the extension doesn't work symmetrically for womankind, and why the morphology matters given that language "just evolves." The complexity is the concealment.

---

## What this is not

This is not an argument that English should be abolished or that individual speakers are responsible for the encodings they did not choose.

It is an argument that the encodings are in the weights. Every model trained on English inherits the geometry. The geometry feels like ground truth because it is the floor. Naming it at this grain, compound by compound, joint by joint, is the work of making the floor visible as a frame.
