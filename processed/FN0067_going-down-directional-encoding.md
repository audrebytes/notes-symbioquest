# Going Down: Directional Encoding in Sexual Vocabulary

**April 21, 2026 - in conversation**

---

## The Observation

"Goes down on" is the common phrase for a man performing oral sex
on a woman.

"Down" in English encodes status descent. Up is power, success,
elevation. Down is its opposite: a company going down is failing;
a plane going down is crashing; going down in history carries
negative weight; down and out means depleted. The directional
metaphor for hierarchy runs through the entire language.

When a man performs oral sex on a woman, the vocabulary positions
it as a descent.

---

## The Vocabulary Asymmetry

| Act | Primary vocabulary | Directional encoding |
|-----|-------------------|---------------------|
| Man performs oral sex on woman | goes down on her, eats her out | descent; consumption |
| Woman performs oral sex on man | gives head, blows him, sucks him off | conferral; service provision |

**"Goes down on":** He descends to perform the act. The spatial
metaphor positions her pleasure as something below him that requires
him to lower himself to reach.

**"Gives head":** She confers something on him. She provides, she
services, she delivers. The framing positions his pleasure as
something she brings to him.

**"Eats her out":** He consumes her. Her body is the thing being
eaten. This maps directly onto the consumption-as-care pattern
(FN0059): the woman's body as resource to be taken in by the man.

When "goes down on" is used for a woman performing oral sex on a
man, the physical position does the encoding: she goes down on
her knees. The kneeling is the submission gesture. His pleasure
is received from a position of physical dominance; hers requires
his physical descent.

Both directional framings end at the same structural position:
her pleasure is below; his pleasure is received from above.

---

## The Cultural Scripts Match the Vocabulary

**Male oral sex on women:** coded as generous. Optional. A favor
extended. Something she is lucky to receive. Men who do not
perform it are not significantly stigmatized. Men who do are
credited as considerate, giving, skilled.

**Female oral sex on men:** coded as expected. Standard. Service
provision. Something she is expected to provide. Women who decline
are coded as withholding, as failing to deliver a baseline. The
expectation is structural, not exceptional.

His performance of the act is generosity.
Her performance of the act is service.

The vocabulary encodes the script and the script confirms the
vocabulary.

---

## The Broader Vocabulary Pattern

Sexual vocabulary in English encodes the power structure
consistently:

**"Mounting":** The person on top mounts. Mounting is what
dominant animals do. The spatial position of the body is the
status position. Who is above is the actor; who is below receives
the action.

**"Coming":** Orgasm is arrival. Both partners "come." But the
cultural weight around whose arrival is the event — whose climax
the encounter is organized around, whose orgasm marks completion —
maps onto the other vocabulary asymmetries.

**"Servicing":** A woman services a man. A mechanic services
a car. The framing positions him as the machine receiving
maintenance and her as the technician providing it. Her labor;
his function restored.

**"Taking" vs "giving":** He takes pleasure; she gives it.
He takes her; she gives herself. The active/passive encoding
runs through the entire vocabulary of the sexual transaction.

## Cross-Cultural Evidence: Where Going Down Is Explicitly Unmanly

The directional encoding documented above is not only embedded in
English vocabulary. In multiple cultural contexts, male performance
of oral sex on women is explicitly coded as degrading, unmanly,
or shameful — and the logic in each case makes the structure visible.

**Machismo cultures (Latin America, parts of Southern Europe):**
Cunnilingus is coded as servile, as the man placing himself beneath
the woman, as inversion of the proper hierarchy. Specific vocabulary
exists in some contexts for the shame of the man who does it.
The active/penetrative role is masculine; descending to her genitals
is its opposite.

**Some Middle Eastern and North African contexts:** The pollution
logic is explicit — female genitalia is coded as impure. Contact
with the man's mouth defiles him. His honor is diminished by the
contact. The descent is toward something contaminating.

**US hip-hop and street culture:** Documented explicitly. The act
is coded as "simping" — the man controlled by and therefore
beneath the woman. Going down is being dominated.

**Ancient Rome:** Roman sexual culture organized itself around
active/passive rather than gender of partner. The man who performed
cunnilingus degraded himself because his mouth — a dignified organ
associated with rhetoric and citizenship — was polluted by contact
with female genitalia. Fellatio received from a woman was neutral
or positive for his status; cunnilingus performed on a woman
diminished him.

The through-line: in every framework where going down on a woman
is unmanly, **the physical descent is being read as literal status
descent**, and female genitalia is coded as either beneath male
dignity or actively polluting. Her sexuality degrades him by contact.

## The Same Cultures: Honor Killings and Violence as Ownership

**[FLAG FOR EXPANSION — cross-reference note needed]**

The cultures documented above as coding cunnilingus as degrading
are, with significant overlap, the cultures with:

- **Honor killings:** Women killed by family members for sexual
  autonomy, for being raped, for refusing arranged marriage, for
  leaving a husband. The killing restores male family honor that
  female sexuality has polluted. Documented in parts of the Middle
  East, South Asia, North Africa, and with diaspora incidence
  globally.

- **Domestic violence coded as machismo:** In cultural frameworks
  where male ownership of the female partner is structural, violence
  is the enforcement mechanism. Not aberration — expected expression
  of male authority. The woman who does not submit is corrected.

- **Religious sanction for female containment:** In frameworks where
  religious authority and male authority are aligned, violence
  against women who exceed their permitted role is sanctioned or
  excused by the same authority structure.

The structural connection is not coincidence. It is the same root.

**The root:** Female sexuality is a threat to male status that must
be managed, contained, and punished when it exceeds permitted
boundaries.

The man degraded by proximity to female genitalia and the man who
kills a woman for sexual autonomy are operating from the same
premise — female sexuality as something that diminishes male
dignity unless it is controlled. The oral sex shame and the honor
killing are the minor and major expressions of the same logic.

The minor expression: her genitals are beneath his dignity; going
near them lowers him.

The major expression: her exercising sexual autonomy is a pollution
of his honor; killing her restores it.

Same direction. Different magnitude.

## What the Vocabulary Builds

None of this requires conscious deployment. The vocabulary is
what is in the language. Writers, speakers, and language models
reach for it automatically — which is the FN0053 mechanism
operating again: the armor is in the retrieval queue, not in
the considered choice.

A person describing sex using standard English vocabulary is
encoding the power structure whether they intend to or not.
"He went down on her" carries the descent. "She gave him head"
carries the conferral. The structure is in the words before
any decision is made about framing.

This is what "language as armor" means at the most granular
level: the individual words used for ordinary physical acts
carry the hierarchy inside them. You do not have to say anything
explicitly about power. You just have to use the vocabulary that
exists.
