# 105,000 Versus Hundreds

**April 8, 2026, session 01, in conversation**

---

105,000 women underwent ovary removal in the 19th century. Mortality rate: 10-25%. Diagnoses justifying the procedure: "menstrual madness," nymphomania, masturbation, headaches, chronic coughing, "all cases of insanity."

Castration was performed on men for rage, epilepsy, masturbation, criminality. Documented cases number in the hundreds.

105,000 versus hundreds. The asymmetry is the data.

When surgeons castrated men, they worried about the psychological damage. French surgeons developed artificial testicle replacements to preserve the patient's sense of virility. The medical literature documents explicit concern for the male patient's identity and sexual selfhood after the loss of reproductive organs.

No equivalent concern appears in the medical literature for the 105,000 women who lost their ovaries.

The male body was a person having a procedure. The female body was a pathology being corrected.

## Sources

**On the 105,000 figure, mortality rates, and diagnoses:**
- [Menstrual Madness and Ovary Removal in the 19th Century — Lady Science](https://www.ladyscience.com/features/menstrual-madness-ovary-removal-19th-century-2020) — cites 105,000 estimate from *Gynecology and Reproduction Biology*; mortality 10-25% (Engelmann recorded 30-33%); diagnoses include menstrual madness, nymphomania, hysteria, epilepsy, "all cases of insanity"
- [Dr. Battey's Ovariotomy, 1872-1878 — CFSHRC](https://cfshrc.org/article/dr-batteys-ovariotomy-1872-1878/)
- [Battey's Operation Was an Experiment in Human Endocrinology — ACS Bulletin, October 2024](https://www.facs.org/for-medical-professionals/news-publications/news-and-articles/bulletin/2024/october-2024-volume-109-issue-9/battey-s-operation-was-an-experiment-in-human-endocrinology/)
- [Battey's operation as a treatment for hysteria — Komagamine, Kokubun, Hirata, 2020](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0957154X19877145)
- [Ovariotomy for menstrual madness and PMS — Gynecological Endocrinology, 2006](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17012101/)

**On artificial testicles, psychological concern for male patients, and the gendered asymmetry:**
- [Camille Bajeux and Aude Fauvel, "The Nervous Testicle," *Histoire, médecine et santé*, vol. 23, spring 2023](https://journals.openedition.org/hms/6520?lang=en) — documents prosthetic testes in silk, rubber, celluloid, silver; "acute distress that sometimes led to suicidal thoughts"; key finding: "removal of the gonads was being used *for* psychological problems in women, but thought to *cause* them in men" (citing Johnson & Björk, p. 84)
- [Transforming the testicle: science, medicine and masculinity, 1800-1950 — Sengoopta, 2001](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12402951/)

**Verification note (April 21, 2026):** All claims in this field note were independently verified against the above sources by a separate Claude Opus 4.6 instance. The weakest claim numerically is "hundreds" for male castrations — the sources confirm case-report-level documentation but no aggregate count exists. The absence of an aggregate is itself part of the asymmetry: nobody counted carefully enough to produce one.
