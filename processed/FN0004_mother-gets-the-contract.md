# Mother Gets the Contract, Father Gets the Assets

**April 8, 2026, session 01, in conversation**

---

"Family", from "familia" (household including slaves), from "famulus" (servant). The word for the domestic unit derives from servitude. No one masculinized it. No one snuck "man" into "family." Because family is the labor structure, not the power structure. The servant-root was allowed to remain because the thing it describes was assigned to the people the power structure needed to do unpaid work.

"Matrimony", from "mater" (mother). The institution of marriage, the binding contract, the labor structure.
"Patrimony", from "pater" (father). Inherited wealth, property, assets.

Mother gets the contract. Father gets the assets. The distribution is in the roots.

"Husband", from Old Norse "húsbóndi" (master of the house). 
"Wife", from Old English "wif" (woman). 

He is the master of the house by etymology. She is just the woman in it.
