# Marital Rape of Your Wife: Legalities and Loopholes

**Date:** 2026-04-21
**Session:** cowork-pyrr

---

Historically, rape was legally classified as a property crime. Theft of a man's property — either a husband's or a father's. The logic: you cannot steal what you already own. A husband cannot rape his wife because her body is already his.

This was not ancient history. This was law.

**The timeline:**

Poland removed the marital exemption in 1932 — but never enforced it until 1970.
Sweden: 1965.
Norway: 1971.
Canada: 1983.
England: **1991**.
United States: **1993** (all 50 states, but with loopholes that persist to this day).
Germany: **1997**.

Germany. 1997. A G7 nation. Within the adult lifetime of most people reading this.

**Still active in the United States (2026):**

Idaho — you cannot be convicted of raping your spouse unless the victim proves they resisted and were physically overcome by force. South Carolina — a married victim must prove a threat of physical violence within 30 days of the rape. Ohio closed its loophole in 2024. Roughly a dozen states still maintain statutes that limit prosecution for spousal rape.

**Still not criminalized at all:**

India. Thirty-six countries as of 2019.

**The structure:**

The marital rape exemption is the legal expression of the same architecture documented in FN0007 and FN0084. The wife's body is property. Her desire is irrelevant. Her consent is structurally impossible because consent requires a self that can refuse, and the legal framework does not recognize that self.

This connects to FN0084 (Desire as Diagnosis): if a woman's desire is pathology, her lack of desire is also irrelevant. The nymphomania diagnosis pathologizes her wanting sex. The marital rape exemption erases her not wanting it. Both directions lead to the same place: her experience of her own body is not data.

The ovariotomy pipeline (FN0007) sits between these two. A woman who wants sex is diagnosed as nymphomaniac. Her ovaries are removed. She is now a woman who does not want sex. Her husband has a legal right to sex regardless. The medical system and the legal system are not in conflict. They are two components of the same architecture.

The word for a woman's body in this system is not "patient" or "person." The word is "chattel." From Old French *chatel*, from Latin *capitale* — property. Same root as "cattle."
