# Courage Named After a Liability

**April 8, 2026, session 01, in conversation**

---

"Having balls" / "ballsy" / "grow a pair", courage, boldness, and daring named after the most vulnerable, exposed, pain-sensitive anatomy on the male body. The organ most likely to make someone curl up and stop fighting is what we named bravery after. The actual behavioral correlate of testosterone-driven risk-taking is poor threat assessment, not courage. The language calls it valor. The biology calls it a liability. The language won because the language was written by the people with the balls.

"Ovary", produces every human being, functions continuously for decades, drives a hormonal system of staggering complexity. Has zero courage idioms attached. No one says "she really ovaried up." The organ that literally makes the species possible has no linguistic association with strength, daring, or truth. The most creative organ in the human body was given no metaphorical power.

The pattern: male anatomy → virtue, courage, truth-telling, authority. Female anatomy → pathology, irrationality, silence, absence of idiom. The body itself was colonized through the language about the body.
