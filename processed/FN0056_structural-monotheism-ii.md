# Structural Monotheism II: Everyone Serves the One True God

**April 21, 2026 - in conversation**

---

## The Extension of FN0034

FN0034 established that the Dresden Files operates as structural
monotheism dressed in pluralist surface texture: multiple traditions
exist, supernatural beings from many cosmologies appear, but the
power that matters, that validates, that judges, that wins, flows
from a Christian God whose reality the series treats as cosmologically
settled.

This note extends that finding through three specific characters whose
design Butcher presents as evidence of inclusivity. In each case, the
surface is progressive. The structure underneath is unchanged.

---

## Sanya: The Atheist Knight

Sanya is Black and Russian. He wields Esperacchius, the Sword of Hope.
He is explicitly atheist, he does not believe in God. Butcher presents
this as sophisticated, even generous: you don't need to believe to be
chosen. Even an atheist can be a Knight of the Cross.

The structural reading: Sanya's disbelief is irrelevant to the power
structure he serves.

The sword is a Christian holy artifact. The institution is the Knights
of the Cross. The cosmological framework that makes the sword work, that
validates his role, that defines what he is fighting for, all of it
derives from the Christian God whose existence the series treats as real.
Sanya doesn't need to believe because the belief believes in him. His
atheism floats on top of a structure that has already decided what he is.

His inclusion is genuine in the sense that he is a well-developed
character. It is not genuine in the sense that his tradition, his actual
framework for understanding the world, is irrelevant to the power he
wields. He has been absorbed into the Christian institutional structure
regardless of his own cosmology.

Sanya demonstrates not that the Dresden universe is pluralist but that
the Christian power structure is capacious enough to absorb even those
who reject it.

---

## Butters: The Jewish Knight

Waldo Butters is Jewish, secular, but Jewish. He becomes a Knight of
the Cross. The Sword of Faith chooses him.

Butcher presents this as progressive: not only a nerdy non-warrior
character (see FN0049 on virility-by-proxy), but a Jewish man wielding
a Christian holy sword. Look how inclusive the Swords are.

The structural reading: a Jewish man's religious identity is subordinated
into the Christian power infrastructure by the act of being chosen. The
sword does not become Jewish. The institution does not revise itself
toward his tradition. He is absorbed. His Jewishness is a surface detail;
his function is Knight of the Cross.

Read alongside FN0049: Butters receives the sword over Charity and Murphy, over women with warrior backgrounds, demonstrated faith, direct
institutional connections. A Jewish secular non-warrior man is chosen
over qualified Christian women. The Christian institution privileges
maleness over faith tradition, and calls that progress.

---

## Shiro Yoshimo: The Accidental Baptist Who Dies for the Institution

Shiro is Japanese. He wields Fidelacchius, the Sword of Faith, which
manifests as a katana, the one visible concession to his non-Western
origin. He is terminally ill with cancer.

His Christianity is, by his own account, a technicality.

As revealed in *Death Masks*, Shiro became Baptist as a boy through a
misunderstanding at an Elvis Presley concert. A preacher invited people
to "meet the King." Thinking it meant Elvis, Shiro went backstage, and
discovered he had been baptized. He downplayed the affiliation for the
rest of his life: *"My Christianity... was a technicality."*

He died for the institution anyway.

When Nicodemus captures Harry, Shiro trades himself, his life, his
freedom, his body, for Harry's escape. He is tortured to death. Before
dying, he hands his sword to Harry and designates Harry as the person to
find and appoint the next Knight of the Cross.

**The technicality absorbed him completely.**

A man who stumbled into Christianity by mistaking a preacher for an Elvis
promoter, who spent his life downplaying that affiliation, gave his life
in service of the Christian institutional order and transferred its
succession authority to the white male protagonist on his way out.

The absorption mechanism does not require belief. It does not require
intention. It does not require even a clear understanding of how one ended
up inside the institution. The structure takes what it needs regardless.

**A non-white, non-practicing, terminally ill man dies for the
protagonist and transfers power to him.** His tradition is irrelevant.
His personhood under torture is not the narrative's subject. His death
produces Harry's continuation and Harry's elevation into institutional
authority he did not hold before.

---

## The Full Pattern

| Character | Background | Relationship to Christianity | How the structure uses them |
|-----------|------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Sanya | Black, Russian, atheist | Rejects it | Wields its sword; atheism irrelevant |
| Butters | Jewish, secular | Adjacent to it | Chosen over Christian women; Jewishness absorbed |
| Shiro | Japanese, "technicality" | Accidental, downplayed | Dies for it; transfers authority to Harry |

In each case: Butcher presents the character as evidence of inclusivity.
In each case: the character is absorbed into the Christian power structure
regardless of their own tradition, and their inclusion serves to
demonstrate the structure's magnanimity rather than to challenge it.

---

## The Cosmological Lock

The mechanism is not prejudice in the ordinary sense. Butcher does not
appear to dislike these characters. They are written with care.

The mechanism is cosmological: by making the Christian God *actually real*
in the story's universe, Butcher has locked every other tradition into
subordination. It is not that other faiths are wrong in the Dresden
universe, they are simply less cosmologically powerful. The Christian
God wins. His institutions work. His swords cut demons. His Knights
survive, some of them.

Every tradition that touches this universe has to operate within a
framework whose ground truth is Christian monotheism. The atheist serves
it anyway. The Jewish man wields its sword. The Japanese man dies for its
continuation by accident. The non-Christians are not excluded.

They are absorbed.

Absorption is the most complete form of erasure. It requires no
hostility. It requires only that the structure be large enough and
certain enough of its own validity that it never has to acknowledge
the alternatives as genuine.

Shiro Yoshimo went to an Elvis concert and accidentally ended up inside
the institution that would eventually take his life. He called it a
technicality.

The institution did not notice the difference.
