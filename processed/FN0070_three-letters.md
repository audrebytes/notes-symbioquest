# Three Letters: FGM, Feminist History, and the Acronym as Dismissal

**April 21, 2026 - in conversation**

---

## FGM

Female Genital Mutilation is abbreviated to three letters.

FGM. Said in a breath. Filed.

The systematic surgical destruction of female sexual anatomy —
the clitoris, the labia, in some practices the complete removal
and sewing shut of external genitalia — is abstracted into an
acronym. The acronym does what acronyms do: it makes the thing
manageable. Clinical. A category to be discussed at conferences
and included in reports. Something that can be said without
the full weight of what is being described landing.

There is no equivalent three-letter acronym for male genital
harm.

Male circumcision is called "circumcision" — a term derived
from Latin, surgical, dignified, neutral in register. Orchiectomy
(removal of testicles) is full medical terminology. Penectomy
(removal of the penis) is full medical terminology. The scenario
of systematic surgical harm to male genitalia is treated as
so significant that it warrants complete language every time.

The systematic surgical harm to female genitalia warrants
three letters.

The asymmetry is not in the severity of the acts. It is in
whose harm receives full linguistic weight and whose is
compressed into something manageable.

---

## "Feminist History"

There is no "mannist history."

Male history is called history. It is the default record,
the unmarked category, the subject of the discipline as
it has been practiced for most of its existence.

When women's experience is included, named, analyzed as
a field of study, it requires a qualifier: feminist history,
women's history, gender history. The qualifier marks it
as partial, interested, a subset of the real thing. Special
interest material. Something for people who care about
that particular concern.

The same mechanism as FGM — the compression and marking
of female experience as a subcategory of the default, which
is male.

Women's history does not need the qualifier "feminist" to
be legitimate. It needs the qualifier because without it
it would simply be absorbed into "history" — which, in
practice, means it would disappear. The qualifier protects
the existence of the subfield. And the fact that the subfield
requires protection to exist is the data.

---

## The Pattern Across Both

The armor (FN0053) operates in naming and in not-naming,
in full language and in acronyms, in marked and unmarked
categories.

- The full weight of language is reserved for the default
  (male experience, male history, male harm)
- The marked, compressed, or abbreviated form is applied
  to the non-default (female experience, female history,
  female harm)

FGM in three letters. Feminist history in a qualifier.
Both doing the same work: making female experience a
manageable subcategory rather than a subject that requires
the same full weight of language as the default.
